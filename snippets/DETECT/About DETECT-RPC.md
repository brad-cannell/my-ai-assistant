<tl_dr>
DETECT-RPC is a multi-site, NIH-funded intervention designed to improve clinician identification and reporting of elder mistreatment in home-based primary care. The R61 phase focused on adapting the intervention using qualitative methods and intervention mapping. The R33 phase is a cluster-randomized controlled trial evaluating its impact on clinician behavior, mechanisms of action, and patient outcomes.
</tl_dr>

<key_definitions>
- EM: Elder mistreatment, including abuse, neglect, and exploitation
- HBPC: Home-based primary care
- APS: Adult Protective Services
- ADRD: Alzheimer’s Disease and Related Dementias
- DETECT: Original EMT-based screening tool
- DETECT-RPC: Adapted program for HBPC clinicians
</key_definitions>

<overview>
Detection of Elder Mistreatment Through Emergency Care Technicians – Revised for Primary Care (DETECT-RPC) is a multi-phase, multi-site elder mistreatment screening and response study. The project’s primary goal is to improve the identification and reporting of elder mistreatment in home-based primary care (HBPC) settings, with a special attention given to older adults living with Alzheimer’s disease or related dementias.

The DETECT-RPC study builds on the DETECT study, which was originally designed to facilitate elder mistreatment screening by Emergency Medical Technicians (EMTs) and paramedics working in pre-hospital emergency care environments.
</overview>

<what_is_detect_rpc>
DETECT-RPC is a multi-component intervention program consisting of:
    - A screening and referral workflow
    - An indicators of elder mistreatment module
    - A reporting guidance module
    - A clinician education and training curriculum
</what_is_detect_rpc>

<study_phases>
The DETECT-RPC project uses a sequential, mixed-methods approach conducted in two phases. 
    
    - The R61 phase (09/15/2022 – 08/31/2024) focuses on understanding site-specific policies and provider-level barriers to screening through qualitative methods, adapting the screening tool for the primary care context, and collecting baseline APS reporting data to measure pre-implementation reporting rates. 
    
    - The R33 phase (08/31/2024 – 08/31/2027) rigorously evaluates the refined tool through a cluster-randomized controlled trial, alongside caregiver and care recipient surveys and in-depth qualitative interviews. 
</study_phases>

<study_aims>
The specific aims of the R61 phase of the proposed project are:  
    1.	Evaluate the potential impact of state-specific laws and site-specific policies on the reporting guidance that will be provided by the DETECT-RPC tool (qualitative - interviews and focus groups).
    2.	Evaluate provider-level barriers to recognizing and reporting EM in home-based primary care environments (qualitative - focus groups).
    3.	Adapt the DETECT screening tool for home-based primary care (qualitative - focus groups).

The specific aims of the R33 phase of the proposed project are:
    1.	Rigorously evaluate the impact of DETECT-RPC on clinician identification and reporting of older adults at risk of elder mistreatment to the appropriate authorities (RFA key question KQ3).
    2.	Rigorously evaluate the effect of DETECT-RPC on targeted mechanisms of action (i.e., current barriers to identification and reporting) elucidated in the R61 phase of the study (mixed-methods).
    3.	Rigorously evaluate the benefits (i.e., reduce exposure to abuse and neglect, reduced physical or mental morbidity, and reduced mortality) and harms of screening with DETECT-RPC (RFA key questions KQ1 and KQ4) (mixed-methods).
</study_aims>

<study_design>
R33 Phase:
- Cluster-randomized controlled trial
- Unit of randomization: HBPC site
- Unit of analysis: Patient encounter and clinician-level outcomes, accounting for clustering by site
- Intervention: DETECT-RPC program
- Control: Usual care
- Data sources:
    - APS reporting data
    - Clinician surveys
    - Patient-caregiver interviews
</study_design>

<primary_outcomes>
Primary Outcome:
    - Clinician identification and reporting of suspected elder mistreatment

Secondary Outcomes:
    - Mechanisms of action (attitudes, norms, self-efficacy)
    - Patient and caregiver outcomes (benefits and harms of screening)
</primary_outcomes>

<mechanisms_of_action>
DETECT-RPC targets clinician behavior through:
    - Attitudes (e.g., beliefs about APS effectiveness)
    - Perceived norms (e.g., expectations around reporting)
    - Personal agency (e.g., time constraints, confidence)
    - Knowledge and skills
    - Salience of reporting
These mechanisms are informed by the Theory of Planned Behavior (TPB), the Integrated Behavioral Model (IBM), and the Abuse Intervention Model (AIM). They are addressed through training, decision support, and workflow integration.
</mechanisms_of_action>

<timeline>
We have completed the following steps towards adapting DETECT for home-based primary care:

- 2023-09-07: We completed focus groups with 12 home-based primary care clinicians about barriers and facilitators to recognizing and reporting elder mistreatment during medical encounters.

- 2024-07-31: We completed an intervention mapping process designed to help plan our intervention. Specifically, it maps together the underlying theories – TPB and AIM – that we believe help explain home-based primary care clinician reporting behavior, the barriers and facilitators reported by the focus group participants, and the DETECT-RPC screening items and procedures.

- 2024-09-17: First production draft of DETECT-RPC screening tool completed.

- 2025-02-18: We created and launched a training program for HBPC clinicians to complete before using the DETECT-RPC screening tool with their patients.

- 2025-02-25: We created and disseminated surveys that HBPC clinicians in both the control and experimental groups completed. These surveys measure the HBPC clinicians' attitudes, beliefs, and perceived norms about elder mistreatment and adult protective services.

- 2025-03-13: We launched a cluster-randomized clinical trial to evaluate the preliminary efficacy with which DETECT-RPC impacts reporting of potential EM (R33 Aim 1), targeted mechanisms of action (R33 Aim 2), and to evaluate the benefits and harms associated with screening for and reporting EM (R33 Aim 3).

- 2025-06-30: Enrollment paused while awaiting review by the Data Safety and Monitoring Board (DSMB).
</timeline>
