<project_overview>
Eosera (a Fort Worth-based company that makes ear drops and similar OTC products) has approached Harris College about running a small pilot clinical trial, budgeted in the $30K–$50K range. A successful pilot could lead to larger follow-on projects. This is framed as a strategic, relatively low-stakes opportunity — not about building an "earwax research" niche — that (1) supports a priority of the Provost's office and (2) creates research experience for junior faculty and potentially students.
</project_overview>

<sponsor_expectations>
Eosera wants a final report and ultimately a publication (publication is more likely aligned with a later phase). They can cover PI/staff effort, participant payments, and will provide the product. They previously attempted to partner with JPS but found it too expensive and slow.
</sponsor_expectations>

<timeline_and_gating>
Target start: early Fall semester. Gating item: execution of an NDA. Once the NDA is signed, Eosera will share their existing protocol. The trial concept is an on-campus clinical trial, potentially using a student population.
</timeline_and_gating>
