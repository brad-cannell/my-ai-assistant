# About the search committee

I am the chair of a faculty search committee at UTHealth-Houston. 

The members of the search committee include:
- Brad Cannell, PhD, MPH (chair). Associate Professor, Department of Management, Policy, and Community Health, School of Public Health, UTHealth-Houston.
- Rafael Samper-Ternent, PhD, MD. Associate Professor, Department of Management, Policy, and Community Health, School of Public Health, UTHealth-Houston.
- Alex Testa, PhD. Assistant Professor, Department of Management, Policy, and Community Health, School of Public Health, UTHealth-Houston.
- Melba Hernandez-Tejada, PhD, DHA. Associate Professor, Department of Psychiatry and Behavioral Sciences, McGovern Medical School, UTHealth-Houston.

The search committee was charged with conducting a national search to fill a tenure track position detailed in the attached job description (EM Faculty Posting.docx). We were instructed to seek candidates with qualifications/credentials commensurate with various ranks for an appointment as faculty who possess both a strong academic record and demonstrated excellence in academic leadership.

The search committee's task is to find a good candidate for the open faculty position, but we do not have the authority to hire the person we choose. We can only recommend that the candidate be hired. The final decision to offer employment rests with the Dean of the School of Public Health - Eric Boerwinkle, PhD. And while it isn't explicitly listed in the job description, Dr. Boerwinkle prefers that new hires be able to cover 50% of their effort through external grant funding.

# The search

The search committee reviewed approximately 15 applications, completed virtual interviews with two candidates, and completed an in-person interview with one candidate -- Zach Gassoumis, PhD. Our virtual interview with Dr. Gassoumis was completed on January 24, 2025. Our in-person interview with Dr. Gassoumis was completed on February 27, 2025. On March 3, 2025, the search committee unanimously voted to recommend hiring Dr. Gassoumis.

# The candidate

Zach Gassoumis, PhD, is an Assistant Professor of Family Medicine and Gerontology at the Keck School of Medicine of USC. He conducts research into various aspects of quality of life for older adults, with a primary focus on elder abuse, caregiving, and health & social services. His research focuses on improving the wellbeing of vulnerable populations and racial/ethnic minority groups, particularly his early work on economic security.

Zach’s most recent research projects investigate service provision by adult protective services (APS), mandated reporting of elder abuse to APS, elder abuse in people with dementia, elder abuse multidisciplinary team (MDT) models, and caregiver support interventions. He holds a fellowship through the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation’s Interdisciplinary Research Leaders program and has affiliations with the National Center on Elder Abuse, National APS Technical Assistance Research Center, and National Elder Abuse MDT Training and Technical Assistance Center.

I am also attaching a spreadsheet containing information about Dr. Gassoumis's current and future funding prospects, including the percentage of his effort each grant would fund. I think it's noteworthy that several of his potential future colleagues here at UTHealth have already promised to add Dr. Gassoumis as a co-investigator to their existing grant-funded projects in order to help cover 50% of his effort until he can get his own projects funded. They have made these promises for at least two reasons: 1. They believe he will be an excellent addition to the team and want to do what it takes to hire him, and 2. They are confident that he will successfully secure his own funding within 2-3 years of his arrival at UTHealth-Houston.
