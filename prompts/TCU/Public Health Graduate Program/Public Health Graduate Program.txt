# About me

My formal training is in epidemiology and gerontology. I am the Eleanor & Murray Zoota Endowed Professor in Neurodegenerative Research and the Associate Dean for Research in the Harris College of Nursing and Health Sciences at Texas Christian University (TCU) in Fort Worth, TX. 

My research focuses on aging, late-life quality of life, and the prevention of elder mistreatment. I have published over 50 peer-reviewed articles and secured more than $30 million in research funding. As the principal investigator of multiple federally funded studies, including the DETECT project, my work has helped screen over 90,000 older adults for potential mistreatment.

In addition to research, I have taught 8 courses at 4 universities and am recognized for my expertise in data science and epidemiologic methods. I have authored two textbook chapters and one textbook and have presented widely on aging-related research and statistical methods. My experience spans scientific writing, grant writing, project management, and public speaking.

# Graduate Program Development at TCU

TCU's Board of Trustees would like TCU to become an R1 research university according to the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education by the 2029 or 2032 rankings.

In response, TCU's provost is asking administrators from across the university to propose new graduate programs. They are especially interested in graduate programs that will:
- Generate revenue with 3-5 years.
- Enhance TCU's national and international reputation.
- Increase externally funded research expenditures at TCU.
- Graduate students with research doctoral degrees.

The goal will be to launch these programs within the next year or so (Fall 2026 or Fall 2027).

# Harris College of Nursing and Health Sciences

Harris College of Nursing and Health Sciences in one of nine colleges at TCU. 

There are nine academic units in Harris College:
- Communication Sciences and Disorders
- Applied Health Sciences
- Kinesiology
- Nursing
- Nurse Anesthesia
- Occupational Therapy
- Social Work
- Physician Associate

There are currently 7 master's degrees offered by the academic units in Harris College:
- MS in Athletic Training
- MS in Bilingual Speech-Language Pathology
- MS in Kinesiology
- MSN in Nurse Educator
- MS in Physician Associate Studies
- MSW in Social Work
- MS in Speech-Language Pathology

There are currently 4 doctoral degrees offered by the academic units in Harris College:
- Doctor of Nursing Practice (DNP)
- PhD in Nursing
- Doctor of Occupational Therapy (OTP)
- PhD in Health Sciences

However, only 2 of the graduate degrees listed above are considered research doctorates.

As the Associate Dean for Research, I've been asked to help develop a proposal for new graduate programs for Harris College. I would like your help.
