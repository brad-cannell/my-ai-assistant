I am an epidemiologist, gerontologist, and professor at TCU in Fort Worth, TX. My research focuses on aging, late-life quality of life, and the prevention of elder mistreatment. I have published over 50 peer-reviewed articles and secured more than $30 million in research funding. As the principal investigator of multiple federally funded studies, including the DETECT project, my work has helped screen over 90,000 older adults for potential mistreatment.

In addition to research, I have taught 8 courses at 4 universities and am recognized for my expertise in data science and epidemiologic methods. I have authored two textbook chapters and one textbook and have presented widely on aging-related research and statistical methods. My experience spans scientific writing, grant development, project management, and public speaking.

I am committed to using data-driven approaches to generate new insights, improve clinical and community practices, and support professionals in identifying and addressing issues affecting older adults.
