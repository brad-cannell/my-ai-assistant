Study Background
I am analyzing data from a study called Link to Care, or L2C for short. People experiencing homelessness and justice involvement (PEH+J) were recruited at a Dallas, Texas, homeless shelter between 2018 and 2022 and randomized into one of three groups: 1) Usual Case Management (UCM) received standard shelter-based case management, 2) UCM+smartphone (UCM+SP) received all UCM components and a smartphone with unlimited calls/texts and a data plan and were prompted to complete one survey on the smartphone each day, and 3) Smartphone-based case management (SPCM), which we also call the Link2Care group, received all UCM+SP components and intervention features including twice weekly prompts that asked if the participant wanted to contact their case manager, and in-app access to Dallas transportation schedules (e.g., bus, train) and helpful websites (i.e., AA/NA meeting finder). 

Participants were supposed to attend 5 visits. Baseline (visit 1) and randomization (visit 2) were a few days apart to prevent excess phone loss. Subsequent visits (visits 3 through 5) were at 1-, 3-, and 6-month intervals post-baseline. Smartphone data were collected using ecological momentary assessments that were prompted daily over the 6-month study period. 

The study hypotheses were:
1. Those assigned to Link2Care would use more care management services than those assigned to the two control conditions.
2. Participants in the SPCM group would have greater reductions in alcohol and drug, and psychological distress compared to participants in the two control groups (i.e., UCM and UCM+SP).
3. Participants in the SPCM group would have greater improvements in quality of life compared to participants in the two control groups (i.e., UCM and UCM+SP).

Measures:
Case Management Use. Case management use and length of sessions are documented in the shelter’s progress notes system. A care manager (blinded to participant condition) extracted the number, length, and date of case management sessions used during the 6-month study period from the shelter’s case management database. These data were aggregated to represent the total number and length (in minutes) of monthly case management sessions completed by each participant.

Timeline followback (TLFB). The timeline followback method was used at visits 1, 3, 4, and 5 to measure alcohol and drug use. At visit 1, we administered a 90-day TLFB for alcohol, drugs, and homelessness. At visit 3, we administered a 30-day TLFB for alcohol, drugs, and homelessness. At visit 4, we administered a 60-day TLFB for alcohol, drugs, and homelessness. At visit 5, we administered a 90-day TLFB for alcohol, drugs, and homelessness.
