# Overview

You are writing a peer-reviewed journal article that outlines the research protocol for your DETECT-RPC study. Summarize the following excerpt from the innovation section of the grant you wrote into a paragraph for the discussion section of the protocol paper.

"The proposed study is novel and innovative in several ways.   

In response to RFA-AG-22-024, this will be the first study conducted, to our knowledge, to adapt and rigorously evaluate an EM screening tool for primary care clinicians that uses systematic observation of the older adult and their environment. A novelty of DETECT-RPC is the focus on a new setting, HBPC, for universal screening of EM. This focus on observational and environmental indicators for EM screening is highly supported by prior research.52–55,57,59 

The proposed study methodology encompasses HBPC practices for more than 2,000 community-dwelling older adults per year (over a thousand of whom are living with ADRD) residing in five geographically diverse metropolitan areas across the United States (i.e., Baltimore, MD; Birmingham, AL; Dallas, TX; Houston, TX; and Palo Alto, CA). The size and scope of this study are such that, if funded, it will represent one of the largest studies of EM screening ever conducted. Thus, this study represents an improvement over previous methodologies, as the large scope of this study will provide robust power to evaluate the proposed hypotheses. 

An innovative element of this clinical trial is the multiple sources of outcome data, including health and social service system records, and interviews with older adults and their caregivers collecting both quantitative data from validated instruments and qualitative data on their experiences. This will provide rigorous and diverse data to evaluate the benefits/harms of screening that will advance scientific and clinical knowledge directly informing the US Preventative Task Force Guidelines."
