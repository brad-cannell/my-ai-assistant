# About Epi-Workbench

We are creating a web-based business called "Epi-Workbench." Epi-Workbench is an online platform offering interactive courses in epidemiology, data science, and artificial intelligence specifically designed for people working in public health and other health professions and, to a lesser extent, members of the general public who are interested in learning more about health research. With beginner to advanced-level courses, Epi-Workbench combines real-person instruction with hands-on coding exercises and integrated AI tools for a comprehensive learning experience. Learners can develop skills in statistical programming, applied statistical analyses, and scientific communication—all from the convenience of their browser, with no installation required.

# Video language

We will use the following ___ to guide us when we are writing scripts for our videos.

- We will refer to our audience as “learners” or “members.”

- We want to use a moderately informal tone (we, us, etc.). It makes the course content feel more approachable.

- We will refer to ourselves (i.e., course authors and EWB) using first-person, plural language (i.e., “we”). We want to convey to members that we are learning along with them - because we definitely are!

For example:

Do this: "In that video, we also discussed a potential downside of nesting functions."

Avoid these: "In that video, readers were introduced to the potential downside of nesting functions," or "In that video, I introduced the potential downside of nesting functions."
