# Background

I am the director of the Research Informatics Core of the UTHealth Cizik Nursing Research Institute. One goal of the core is to build an informatics infrastructure for the Cizik School of Nursing, and UTHealth at large. That includes access to tools, data, and training. 

As part of that effort, we need to understand two basic things at this point: 1. What data do we already have, and 2. What data and informatics tools do our faculty need? To get us started, we are creating a survey that we will send out to faculty, and perhaps some other key stakeholders, at UTHealth.

One of my GRA's, Morri, already took a shot at creating a survey, but the end result didn't meet our needs. Below is the feedback I received from Dr. Carolyn Pickering, the director of the CNRI about the survey Morri created:

"Thanks for your hard work on this. I especially appreciate your attention to detail with information about the questionpro programming. 

I met with several faculty from our target audience and the general feedback is that this is much too "high-level". This is true both for our clinical faculty and our research faculty. I do appreciate some of the questions offer definitions for terms, but the definitions still assume the reader has a more advanced level of knowledge in regard to data science. The majority of our research faculty and clinical faculty do not have this knowledge, and this entire idea of creating a repository will be new to them. Many don't know how a data repository could help them. It may be helpful to learn more about end goals for the user (how a data resource could help their scholarship or research) and use a similar framing to help understand needs more generally that could be extrapolated to designing a data repository rather than the more specific questions about system functionality. For example: would being able to see a table or visual representation of data be useful for you? Rather than the diagram of the data flow. We also don't need a demographics form, and including such information would require this to go through IRB as a quality improvement project rather than an administrative needs assessment survey."
